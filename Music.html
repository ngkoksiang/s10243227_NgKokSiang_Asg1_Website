Michael Jackson

Michael Joseph Jackson was an American singer, composer, dancer, and philanthropist who lived from August 29, 1958, through June 25, 2009. He is acknowledged as one of the most influential cultural leaders of the 20th century and is known as the "King of Pop." His contributions to dance, fashion, music, and his publicized personal life over the course of a four-decade career elevated him to a universally recognized figure in popular culture. Jackson had an impact on musicians in a variety of musical genres. Through his stage and video appearances, he popularized difficult dance techniques like the robot and the moonwalk, which he also gave their names to. He has received the most awards in music history.

Favourite Songs

| Beat It | The album's third single, "Beat It," was made available on February 14, 1983. It spent three weeks at the top of the Billboard Hot 100 chart, where it reached its pinnacle.  It peaked at the top of the Billboard Hot Black Singles chart as well.  The song was reached No. 5 by Billboard in 1983.  The American Recording Industry Association has awarded it an 8-platinum certification (RIAA). It is among the top-selling singles of all time, with over 11 million copies sold globally.

| Billie Jean | Released by Epic Records on January 2, 1983, as the second single from his sixth studio album, Thriller (1982).


| Man In The Mirror | released on February 6, 1988, as the fourth single from Jackson's seventh solo album, Bad (1987)."Man in the Mirror" topped the Billboard Hot 100 for two weeks, becoming Jackson's tenth number-one single on the chart, and was certified 3× Platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).
